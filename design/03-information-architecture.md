# 03 — Information Architecture

The current site is a product brochure. The redesign is a **trust argument** that
moves the reader from *"I have an error/quality problem"* to *"Alpha is the
proven, credible partner who fixes it — let me talk to them."*

## Narrative arc

```
Promise  →  Stakes  →  Proof  →  Method  →  Mechanisms  →  Products
   →  Track record  →  Voices  →  Authority  →  Standard  →  Act
```

Each section has exactly one job and one emotional beat. Every section offers the
same single next step: **Request a free consultation.**

### The spine: an RFT journey from mediocre to world‑class

A single device threads the whole page — a **Right‑First‑Time ladder** the reader
climbs as they scroll. It makes the two altitudes (doc 01 §1a) physical:

```
 45–60% ───────────▶ 90–92% ───────────▶ 94–96%
 mediocre            great                world-class
 │  THE BIG LEAP        │  THE FINAL MILE       │
 │  efficiency-first,   │  premium craft,       │
 │  fix the vital 20%   │  land tier-1 brands   │
 └─ Calibration feel ───┴─ Atelier feel ────────┘
```

- The **Big Leap** is the workhorse, foregrounded most of the page: "we'll take
  your factory from bleeding rework to genuinely great." Engineered, evidence‑led.
- The **Final Mile** is the summit, reserved for the proof, the standard, and the
  CTA: "and we'll get you the last points that win Nike/Adidas/Uniqlo/Decathlon."
- The ladder reappears as a motif (hero metric, Proof, Method, the Standard) so
  the reader always knows where they are on the climb.

## Section-by-section plan

> Section numbers below are narrative order, not the old "01–05" decorative
> numbering. We keep a numbering motif (it reads as precision) but re‑sequence.

### 1. Hero — *The Promise*
- **Job:** In one screen, say what Alpha does and the outcome it produces.
- **Content:** Precision/RFT headline; one‑sentence value prop; primary CTA
  (consultation); secondary CTA (see the proof / scroll). Optional restrained
  **metric strip** (e.g. *RFT 80%→90%*, *28 yrs textile expertise*, *3 factories
  live*) — evidence in the first viewport.
- **Beat:** confidence. *"These people are exact."*
- **Keep:** full‑bleed treatment + the existing AVIF/preload performance pattern.

### 2. The Stakes — *Why precision decides who wins* (NEW)
- **Job:** Frame the problem in the buyer's terms — errors cap you at commodity
  tier; precision unlocks brand‑tier contracts.
- **Content:** The cost of getting it wrong (rework, rejects, lost trust, capped
  margins) vs. what consistent RFT unlocks. Short, sharp, no product talk yet.
- **Beat:** recognition. *"That's exactly my problem."*

### 3. The Proof — *Results first* (ELEVATED from old section 6)
- **Job:** Earn the right to be believed before explaining how.
- **Content:** Lead with the **Big Leap** headline metric — factories taken from
  **45–60% → 90–92% RFT** (mediocre → great). Then the **Final Mile** evidence —
  Spica **90% → 95% by volume**, targets **93% / 98%** (great → world‑class).
  Tabular figures, large. Both altitudes, in order.
- **Beat:** belief. *"This actually works — at scale, with numbers."*

### 4. The Method — *The Right‑First‑Time Method* (NEW, ownable, two‑stage)
- **Job:** Show Alpha is a system of improvement that operates at both altitudes.
- **Content:** Present the journey as two stages on the RFT ladder:
  - **Stage 1 — The Big Leap (Pareto / efficiency‑first):** find the vital 20% of
    error sources causing 80% of the loss → eliminate at the source → lock the
    gain → climb. This is where most factories make their jump to ~90%.
  - **Stage 2 — The Final Mile (premium craft):** systematically close the last
    defects others accept, pushing 90% → 94–96% — the points that win brand‑tier
    contracts.
- **Beat:** trust in approach. *"They optimize big — and they can finish the job."*

### 5. The Mechanisms — *Errors eliminated* (REFRAME of old Overview + Benefits)
- **Job:** Make the capabilities concrete as *errors killed → standards met*.
- **Content:** A matrix/cards: each error source → how Alpha eliminates it → the
  standard it satisfies. Folds the old "Overview" features and "Benefits" into one
  evidence‑led grid (recipe automation, guided weighing, lot‑drift compensation,
  enforced process path, equipment integration/retrofit, ease of use).
- **Beat:** understanding. *"I see exactly how they do it."*

### 6. The Products — *Alpha Smart Dyehouse & Alpha Dyes Weighing* (KEEP, reframed)
- **Job:** For the reader who wants depth, the actual products.
- **Content:** Keep the two‑product accordion pattern; rewrite intros to lead with
  the error/standard outcome. Retain accordion deep‑link from nav.
- **Beat:** substance. *"Real, mature products."*

### 7. The Track Record — *Factories already live* (KEEP, strengthen)
- **Job:** Prove it's deployed in real factories.
- **Content:** Tuấn Hồng / Dũng Đông / Spica with year + delivered components.
  Keep partner‑logo cards. Consider a quiet timeline (2022 → 2024) to show
  momentum.
- **Beat:** social proof. *"Established companies trust them."*

### 8. The Voices — *Testimonials* (KEEP, promote metrics)
- **Job:** Human, credible, specific endorsement.
- **Content:** The two real testimonials with photos; pull the RFT/error quotes
  forward. Keep schema.org Review/AggregateRating + translated‑review disclaimer.
- **Beat:** reassurance. *"People like me vouch for them."*

### 9. The Authority — *Founder story* (KEEP, elevate as trust anchor)
- **Job:** Establish rare, deep domain credibility.
- **Content:** Phan Đức Tuấn Anh — 28 yrs, 13 dyeing technician + 15 factory
  director, Organic Chemistry. This is *why* Alpha understands errors others miss.
- **Beat:** authority. *"They've actually run a dye house."*

### 10. The Standard — *Brand‑tier readiness* (NEW, careful)
- **Job:** Connect precision to the aspiration — bigger customers.
- **Content:** "Meet the precision standards brands like Nike, Adidas, Uniqlo and
  Decathlon demand of their suppliers." **Words, not logos.** Strictly within the
  guardrail in doc 01 §7.
- **Beat:** aspiration. *"This is my path to the big leagues."*

### 11. The Call — *Consultation CTA* (KEEP, strengthen)
- **Job:** Convert. One clear action.
- **Content:** "Request a free consultation," phone + email (keep copy‑to‑clipboard
  email UX). Restate the precision promise once more.
- **Beat:** decision. *"Let me talk to them."*

### 12. Footer / Contact (KEEP)
- Legal name, phone, email, socials, copyright. Keep structured contact data.

## Navigation

- Sticky top nav, transparent over hero → solid on scroll (keep existing behavior).
- Re‑label nav to the new IA: **Why Precision · Proof · Method · Products ·
  Clients · About · Contact** (final labels tuned to fit; keep products dropdown
  deep‑linking into the accordion).
- Keep the language switcher (VI/EN) and skip‑link.
- Maintain one persistent primary CTA reachable from anywhere.

## Conversion design

- **One** primary action site‑wide: *Request a free consultation.* Repeat it; do
  not introduce competing CTAs.
- Every section ends pointing forward (toward the next beat or the CTA).
- Reduce friction: phone (tap‑to‑call) and email (one‑tap copy) always one action
  away.

## What changes structurally vs. the old site

| Old | New |
|---|---|
| Hero: "Digital Transformation" | Hero: precision/RFT promise + proof strip |
| (none) | **Stakes** section — frame the problem |
| Reviews buried at #6 | **Proof** promoted near the top |
| (none) | **Method** — named Right‑First‑Time / Pareto approach |
| Overview + Benefits (two grids) | Merged into **Mechanisms** (errors → eliminated → standard) |
| Products / Projects / Reviews / Story | Kept, reframed, re‑sequenced for the arc |
| (none) | **The Standard** — brand‑tier aspiration (guardrailed) |

## Constraints carried forward (non‑negotiable)

- Bilingual EN/VI parity for every string (`_data/i18n/*.yml`).
- Preserve schema.org microdata, hreflang, OG/Twitter, sitemap.
- Preserve the performance posture (LCP preload, AVIF, deferred JS, lean CSS).
- Accessibility: landmarks, focus, contrast, reduced‑motion, forced‑colors.
