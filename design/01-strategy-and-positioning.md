# 01 — Strategy & Positioning

## 1. The reframe

The current site is **product‑led**: "We build factory‑management and automation
software (digital transformation)." That is *what Alpha makes*. It is not *what
the customer buys*.

A dye‑house owner does not wake up wanting "digital transformation." They wake up
wanting **bigger, better‑paying, more secure contracts** — and the thing standing
between them and those contracts is **inconsistent quality and errors**.

So the redesign leads with the outcome, not the tool:

> **Old:** Alpha Software — Digital Transformation for Textile Factories.
> **New:** Alpha Software — **Precision is the business.** We make every batch
> right the first time, so your factory earns the trust of the world's most
> demanding brands.

The software and hardware (Alpha Smart Dyehouse, Alpha Dyes Weighing) are the
*means*. The product we actually sell is **precision → reliability → credibility
→ access to tier‑1 customers.**

### 1a. The two altitudes of value (this is the core organizing idea)

Alpha wins at **two altitudes**, and the site must communicate both clearly —
because they speak to two different fears and ambitions in the buyer.

| | **The Big Leap** *(most of the time)* | **The Final Mile** *(premium)* |
|---|---|---|
| RFT journey | **45–60% → 90–92%** | **90–92% → 94–96%** |
| Promise | "We optimize big. We'll save your factory." | "We get you the last points others can't." |
| Method | Efficiency‑first: fix the **vital 20%** of error sources that cause **80%** of the loss | Premium craft: chase the final defects others accept and "call it a day" |
| Emotional beat | *mediocre → great* | *great → world‑class* |
| Who it's for | A factory bleeding rework, stuck at commodity tier | A good factory that wants tier‑1 brand contracts |
| Aesthetic | **Calibration** — engineered, exact, evidence‑led | **Atelier** — material, elevated, crafted |

> **Most of the time we lean into the Big Leap** — abundant, hard‑won expertise
> applied to the obvious, expensive problems for outsized returns. But we can also
> deliver the **Final Mile**: pushing to 94–96% RFT where competitors stop at
> 85–92% and call it done. That last stretch is precisely what lets a factory
> land deals with important international clients and major brands.

The website's structure mirrors this: a clear **RFT journey** from mediocre to
world‑class, with the Big Leap as the workhorse narrative and the Final Mile as
the aspirational summit. (See the "RFT ladder" device in doc 03.)

## 2. Why precision is the whole game (the customer's logic)

Tier‑1 buyers — Decathlon, Uniqlo, Nike, Adidas and the brands like them — do not
choose suppliers on price alone. They audit relentlessly on:

- **Consistency** — every batch matches the standard and the last batch.
- **Right‑First‑Time (RFT)** — low rework, low reject, predictable output.
- **Traceability & process control** — provable, repeatable processes.
- **On‑time, in‑full delivery** — which is impossible without RFT.

A Vietnamese dye house that can *prove* high, stable RFT and tight process control
becomes **audit‑ready** and moves up the value chain — from commodity work to
premium, long‑term brand contracts. A factory that can't stays stuck competing on
price.

**Precision is therefore not a feature. It is the gate.** Everything Alpha does —
recipe automation, guided weighing, enforced process paths, equipment integration
— exists to move a factory through that gate. That is the spine of the new site.

## 3. The method we can name and own (Pareto)

Alpha's approach is itself a selling point, and the customer named it: **start by
eliminating the 20% of error sources that cause 80% of the defects, then work
upward.** This is honest, credible, and differentiating — it says Alpha is a
*system of improvement*, not a box of software.

Name it on the site as a clear, ownable method. Working name:

> **The Right‑First‑Time Method** — find the vital few error sources, eliminate
> them at the source, lock the gain into the process, then climb.

The error sources Alpha attacks first (the "vital 20%"), all already in the
content, reframed as *errors killed*:

| Error source (the defect generator) | How Alpha eliminates it |
|---|---|
| Wrong / inconsistent dye recipe | Software stores expert technology, auto‑generates the recipe — one click, same recipe every time |
| Dye weighing mistakes | QR‑code dye identification + voice‑guided weighing + multi‑scale support |
| Dye‑lot color‑strength drift (3–5%) | Software auto‑compensates so a new lot doesn't shift the color |
| Missing / extra / wrong process steps | Software‑enforced technological path — no batch runs incomplete or out of order |
| Manual machine operation error | Retrofit manual machines to automatic, software‑controlled programs |
| Subjective color assessment | Integration with Datacolor spectrophotometers & lab dispensers |

Each becomes a "this error → eliminated → this standard now met" story. Evidence,
not adjectives.

## 4. Proof points (already real — surface them, don't bury them)

The current site hides its best assets in section 6. The redesign promotes them.

- **Spica:** RFT rose **80% → 90% per batch** and **90% → 95% by volume**;
  targets of **93% / 98%** in sight. *(quantified, named, dated — gold)*
- **Three real factory clients:** Tuấn Hồng (2022), Dũng Đông (2023), Spica (2024)
  — with the exact components delivered. A track record, not a promise.
- **Two named testimonials with photos**, both emphasizing error reduction and
  process control.
- **Founder authority:** Phan Đức Tuấn Anh — **28 years** in textiles (13 as a
  dyeing technician, 15 as a factory director), background in Organic Chemistry.

**Rule:** lead with evidence early and often. Numbers, names, photos, dates.

## 5. Audience

| Priority | Who | What they feel | What converts them |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Dye‑house / textile‑mill **owners & directors** in Vietnam | Stuck at commodity tier; want bigger brand contracts | Proof of RFT gains + path to audit‑readiness |
| Secondary | **Chief engineers / technical managers** | Pain of errors, rework, dependence on a few skilled experts | Mechanisms that eliminate errors and reduce skill dependence |
| Tertiary | **Quality / compliance managers** | Preparing for brand audits | Traceability, process control, consistency |

All three share one motivator: **error costs me money and credibility; precision
earns me both back.**

## 6. Messaging hierarchy

**Primary slogan (recommended):**
> **Precision is the business.**
> Right the first time, every time — so the world's most demanding brands can
> count on you.

**Supporting one‑liners (rotate across sections / meta / OG):**
- "From dye house to world‑class supplier."
- "Eliminate the errors that cost you contracts."
- "Make every batch audit‑ready."
- "We don't chase perfection. We engineer it."

**Value proposition (the elevator version):**
> Alpha turns your factory's hard‑won expertise into software that applies the
> right technology to every batch automatically — eliminating the errors that
> cause rework and rejects, raising Right‑First‑Time, and giving you the
> consistent, provable quality that premium global brands require.

**Tone of voice:** Precise, calm, confident, evidence‑driven. An expert engineer,
not a hype salesperson. Short declarative sentences. Quantify claims. Never
overpromise — credibility *is* the product.

## 6a. Brand personality & identity (the design must embody these)

Identity is the most important thing in the design. The design language must never
contradict who Alpha is:

- **Standards‑respecting.** Alpha understands what standards (ISO, industry, web)
  mean and how they help. The *design itself* therefore follows standard, proven
  web practice — what 95% of users would call "natural" and "intuitive." Adhering
  to standards is a message: it signals discipline, authority, and expertise.
  → clean, conventional, accessible, correct; no gimmicks for their own sake.
- **Authoritative, professional, expert.** Restraint over flash. Confident
  typography, generous whitespace, evidence over decoration.
- **Personal & human‑centred.** Alpha spends **months** surveying each factory to
  understand it deeply and build software for *that specific* factory, and improves
  the product continuously through **active collaboration** with everyone — line
  workers, skilled workers, professionals, managers, leadership. *Factories are
  made of and run by humans; Alpha looks past the systems to see the people.*
  → the design is warm and human, not cold/sterile tech: real faces and voices
  (founder + client portraits, testimonials) carry weight; approachable clarity;
  the customer is a partner, not a transaction.
- **Pragmatic & practical.** Function over cleverness; clarity over ornament;
  solve the real problem.

> Design test: *does this choice read as standard, professional, human, and
> precise — and would a careful factory director trust it?* If not, change it.
> φ (the golden ratio) is a **final splash**, never the backbone (see design/04).

## 7. The brand‑aspiration guardrail (non‑negotiable)

Decathlon, Uniqlo, Nike, Adidas are referenced as the **standard to reach**, never
as clients or endorsers.

- ✅ "Meet the precision standards that brands like Nike, Adidas, Uniqlo and
  Decathlon demand of their suppliers."
- ✅ "Become the kind of supplier a Decathlon or a Uniqlo can rely on."
- ❌ Anything implying these brands are Alpha customers, partners, or endorsers.
- ❌ Using their logos in a way that implies association. If brand names appear,
  they appear as *words in a sentence about standards*, not as a logo wall.

This honesty is on‑brand: the product's whole premise is traceable, provable
truth. The marketing must match it.

## 8. SEO / discoverability continuity

Keep the existing technical SEO (schema.org, hreflang, OG/Twitter, sitemap) and
**evolve the keywords** from generic automation toward the precision/outcome
vocabulary buyers actually search:

- right‑first‑time textile dyeing, RFT improvement, dyeing quality consistency,
  reduce dyeing defects/rework, dye recipe management software, color matching
  spectrophotometer integration, textile supplier audit readiness, dye house
  process control, automatic dye weighing.

Every new line of copy is authored in **both EN and VI** (`_data/i18n/en.yml` and
`vi.yml`) — bilingual parity is a hard requirement, not an afterthought.
