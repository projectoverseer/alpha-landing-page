The hub still opens straight on the newest post (protected, philosophy §8), and the topic strip that once sat above the feed was removed by the owner — this block does not bring it back. It waits where the feed runs out: the reader who scrolled the archive is exactly the one ready to be handed a structured path instead of an end-of-list dead end. One card per series, whole card one link to the series page. Each card says what the path teaches (the series desc, the same single-source line the series page and its meta description use) and what it costs (n parts, total minutes) — a finishable course is an attractive one. Plain HTML in document order: while the feed's drip is running the rail sits after the sentinel, and the IntersectionObserver's 600px look-ahead loads the remaining batches before the reader ever reaches it, so it renders at the true end of the feed. With JS off it is simply the last thing after the complete list. Crawlers see every series from the hub root. {%- endcomment -%}
